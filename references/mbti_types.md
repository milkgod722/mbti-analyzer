## Cognitive Function Stacks

Each MBTI type uses a specific "stack" of cognitive functions in order of preference:
- **Dominant (Dom)**: Your strongest, most natural function
- **Auxiliary (Aux)**: Supports the dominant, provides balance
- **Tertiary (Tert)**: Less developed, emerges later in life
- **Inferior (Inf)**: Weakest area, source of stress and growth potential

## Dimension Definitions

### EI — Extraversion vs Introversion
- **E (Extraversion)**: Draw energy from external stimulation, social interaction, and engaging with the world outside.
- **I (Introversion)**: Draw energy from internal reflection, prefer depth over breadth, find alone time recharging.

### SN — Sensing vs Intuition
- **S (Sensing)**: Focus on concrete facts, details, and real-world data. Prefer practical, hands-on learning.
- **N (Intuition)**: Focus on patterns, possibilities, and abstract connections. Prefer imaginative, conceptual thinking.

### TF — Thinking vs Feeling
- **T (Thinking)**: Make decisions through logical analysis, objective criteria, and consistency.
- **F (Feeling)**: Make decisions through personal values, empathy, and the human impact on others.

### JP — Judging vs Perceiving
- **J (Judging)**: Prefer structure, planning, and organization. Like to make decisions and resolve things quickly.
- **P (Perceiving)**: Prefer flexibility, spontaneity, and keeping options open. Comfortable with ambiguity.

## Report Interpretation Guide

### Score Moderate (40-60%)
A moderate score indicates a **balanced** preference. The person can adapt to both sides of the dimension depending on context. In reports, note this as "moderate" and describe how they might flex between both preferences.

### Score Slight to Moderate (35-65%)
Slight-to-moderate preferences indicate someone who leans but isn't strongly committed to one side. Avoid deterministic language.

### Score Strong (20-80% and beyond)
Strong preferences indicate deeply ingrained patterns. The person's behavior will consistently reflect this preference, especially under stress or fatigue.

### Why These Pairings Work

**Ideal Matches (Golden Pairs)**:
- Share the same middle two letters (S/N and T/F) but differ on E/I and J/P
- Examples: INTJ-ENTP, INFJ-ENFP, ISTJ-ESTP

**Pedagogical Pairs (Growth Partners)**:
- Opposite types that challenge each other to grow
- Examples: INTJ-ESFP, INFJ-ESTP
- Requires maturity and mutual respect to thrive

### Communication Dimension Patterns

**E vs I**:
- **E**: Thinks out loud, processes externally, energized by discussion
- **I**: Reflects before speaking, prefers one-on-one, needs time to formulate thoughts

**S vs N**:
- **S**: Wants concrete examples, step-by-step details, practical applications
- **N**: Prefers concepts, big picture, theoretical implications

**T vs F**:
- **T**: Values logic, objectivity, may seem detached (but cares in their way)
- **F**: Values harmony, personal impact, may prioritize relationships over facts

**J vs P**:
- **J**: Appreciates agendas, closure, clear decisions
- **P**: Prefers flexibility, exploration, keeping options open
